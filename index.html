AI Initiative Delivery
JD: "AI teams · model validation · experimentation · analytics-driven"
AI delivery isn't regular software delivery with a different backlog. Models fail in ways stories don't. Validation pipelines have different rhythms than CI/CD. Experimentation requires safety to fail fast. I've facilitated this specific context.
From: Stealth AI Startup — 2024 Led Agile ceremonies for 2 concurrent AI technical teams building data pipelines and automation workflows. Incorporated model validation gates, data access constraints, and security review SLAs directly into Definition of Ready — so nothing entered a sprint that would stall waiting for compliance approval. Fostered psychological safety for teams to surface failed model runs in retrospectives as learning artifacts, not failures to explain.
🧠
Psychological Safety in AI Teams
JD: "experimentation and iteration · shared accountability · safety to fail"
AI teams that can't fail safely can't iterate. Psychological safety isn't a culture poster — it's an SM discipline, built through consistent retrospective practice and by how you respond when a model run fails in sprint.
How I build it In retros I ask: "What did we learn from the failed experiment?" not "Why did the model miss targets?" The framing shifts accountability from individual to system. I model vulnerability first — sharing what I got wrong as SM before asking the team. By sprint 6, teams I join score higher on psychological safety than sprint 1. Not because of a workshop. Because of 6 sprints of consistent behavior.

Regulated Environment Delivery
JD: "regulatory compliance · security reviews · compliance considerations in DoR"
Regulated delivery means compliance isn't a step after development — it's a gate before a story enters sprint. I've built DoR criteria in environments where stories that skipped compliance review created audit findings, not just bugs.
From: DigitasLBI / SunTrust Banks — 2014 Embedded SM in a heavily regulated financial services transformation. Incorporated security review checkpoints, compliance sign-off requirements, and data handling documentation directly into Definition of Ready. Stories that couldn't meet regulatory acceptance criteria didn't enter planning — full stop. Maintained audit-ready Jira documentation and Confluence records that survived external review. Direct analog to insurance/claims compliance requirements.

Transparent Risk Dashboards
JD: "velocity · throughput · cycle time · unplanned work % · risk exposure"
The JD names a specific metrics set. Here's the exact framework I use — not just the metrics, but what each one signals and when it triggers action.
VELOCITY
Trend over 5+ sprints. Point-in-time is noise. Declining trend = system problem, not capacity problem.
THROUGHPUT
Stories completed per sprint regardless of size. Low throughput + high velocity = oversized stories. Fix the backlog.
CYCLE TIME
Days from in-progress to done. Spikes usually reveal hidden dependencies or review bottlenecks — not slow engineers.
UNPLANNED WORK %
In regulated environments this is the key signal. >20% consistently = broken intake process or stakeholder boundary issue.
